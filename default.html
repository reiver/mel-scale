Mel Scale

an audio frequency scale that tries to mimic how the human ear perceives pitch

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux

The mel scale is used when creating a mel spectrogram from a regular Spectrogram.

Alternatives

There are some alternatives to the mel scale. These include:

Scale Name Type Common Usages
Mel Perceived pitch Speech recognition, MFCCs
Bark Critical bands Psychoacoustics, audio coding
ERB Auditory filter bandwidths Hearing research, auditory models
Greenwood Cochlear anatomy Cochlear modeling
Log-frequency Musical pitch Music analysis
Constant-Q Musical intervals MIR, transcription
Gammatone + ERB Auditory physiology Bio-inspired audio processing

For speech recognition, machine learning and AI, from a practical point of view the most common alternatives to mel scale filterbanks are gammatone + ERB filterbanks, bark-scale filterbanks, and sometimes learned filterbanks where an artificial neural network discovers its own frequency representation directly from audio.
